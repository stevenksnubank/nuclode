# Find Counterarguments

Search available knowledge sources for people who have argued against a given claim, and report their arguments with context and links.

## Steps

1. **Decompose the claim** into searchable keywords. Identify:
   - The core assertion
   - Key domain terms
   - Likely opposing positions (what would someone say if they disagreed?)

2. **Search internal sources** (if available): Use Glean, Confluence MCP, or similar tools to find conversations where people discuss the topic. Try multiple query variations:
   - Direct keywords from the claim
   - Terms associated with the opposing view
   - Related debates or decisions

3. **Search external sources**: Use WebSearch to find public counterarguments — blog posts, conference talks, HN/Reddit discussions, academic papers.

4. **Read promising results**: For results that look relevant, get the full context. Look for:
   - Explicit disagreements with the claim
   - Alternative approaches that implicitly challenge the claim
   - Trade-off discussions where the opposite position was considered
   - Decision records where the claim was debated

5. **Report findings**: For each counterargument found, report:
   - **Who** made the argument (name, role, or community)
   - **Where** they made it (link to thread, page, or post)
   - **Key quote** — the most relevant passage in their own words
   - **Strength** — how directly it opposes the claim (direct rebuttal, alternative approach, qualified disagreement)

If no counterarguments are found, say so explicitly and suggest:
- Alternative search terms to try
- Whether the claim might be uncontroversial or too niche to have been debated
